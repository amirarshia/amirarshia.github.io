Sea turtles live/environment


Sea turtles are amazing creatures. They come in different colors, and sizes;
these gentle giants can weights up to 2,000 LBS 900 kilograms, (unit of
mass in the metric system),and when they are hatched from their egg,
they can be as small as the palm of your hand. They also mostly travel
in warmer parts of the oceans like in the Indian ocean and the Pacific
ocean. Sea turtles have also been on earth for more than 100 million
years, that also means they shared the earth with dinosaurs at one point.

Nesting


When it is time for the sea turtles to reproduce, the female sea turtles take
a trip to the shoreline to lay their eggs. Females(a girl) come out of sea to
nest eggs while the male(a boy) turtles stay in the sea. Sea turtles dig with
their back flippers, nest their eggs, and cover them again. And also, they
mostly come back to the same beach. They lay 110 eggs each time!!

survival

Sea turtles protect themselves in many ways. They don’t get in danger that
often but if they do, they have very strong flippers, so they can have
a fast runaway. And also, they have a sleek body that protects them.
They don’t have teeth but, their jaws have modified (make partial or
minor changes to something) “beaks” to help them eat their usual diet
which could be anything from seaweed to jelly fish. they search for
food all day, Sea turtles are like pigs, they eat a lot of food!
